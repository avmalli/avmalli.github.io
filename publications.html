Mallikarjun, A., Shroads, E., & Newman, R. S. (2024). Perception of vocoded speech in domestic dogs. Animal Cognition, 27(1), 34.

This study examined how word recognition changes when speech is systematically degraded. By reducing acoustic information, we identified which cues are critical for recognition and explored whether similar strategies are used across species.

Gokool, V. A., Crespo-Cajigas, J., Mallikarjun, A., Collins, A... & Otto, C. M. (2017). The use of biological sensors and instrumental analysis to discriminate COVID-19 odor signatures.Biosensors, 12(11), 1003-1028.

We examined whether volatile organic compound (VOC) profiles from worn T-shirts could differentiate COVID-19 positive and negative individuals using both trained detection dogs and HS-SPME-GC-MS instrumental analysis. The findings support a combined biological and analytical approach to developing noninvasive, translational disease screening tools.

DOI PDF Project

Mallikarjun, A., Collins, A., Verta, A., Hanadari-Levy, A., Chaskes, M. B., Rosen, M. R., ... & Otto, C. M. (2023). Canine scent detection of sinonasal-inverted papilloma in blood plasma and nasal secretions. Journal of Veterinary Behavior, 62, 29-38.

We tested whether VOC profiles in blood can differentiate individuals with sinonasal inverted papilloma (SNIP) from healthy controls using biosensors (trained detection dogs), and examined how that VOC signature generalizes to nasal secretions. The findings contribute to the development of noninvasive screening methods for SNIP.

DOI PDF Project

Tsui, A. S. M., Erickson, L. C., Mallikarjun, A., Thiessen, E. D., & Fennell, C. T. (2020). Dual language statistical word segmentation in infancy: Simulating a language-mixing bilingual environment. Developmental Science, 24(3), e13050.

Infants were tested on their ability to segment words from two interleaved artificial language streams with overlapping syllables. Extending my undergraduate thesis work on statistical learning, this study shows that phonetic cues help infants learn from mixed language speech input, which demonstrates how learners might manage competing statistical structures early in development

DOI PDF Project

Mallikarjun, A., Shroads, E., & Newman, R. S. (2019). The cocktail party effect in the domestic dog (Canis familiaris). Animal Cognition, 22(3), 423-432.

We examined whether dogs can recognize their name in multitalker noise using a paradigm originally developed for infants. Dogs succeeded at noise levels where one-year-old infants typically fail, helping to disentangle auditory and attentional factors from language-specific knowledge in speech-in-noise perception.

DOI PDF Project

Mallikarjun, A., Newman, R. S., & Novick, J. M. (2017). Exploiting the interconnected lexicon: Bootstrapping English language learning in young Spanish speakers. Translational Issues in Psychological Science, 3(1), 34.

Review and synthesis comparing psycholinguistic evidence on bilingual language learning with prevailing pedagogical assumptions. Bridges empirical research and educational practice, highlighting where evidence supports or challenges common instructional models.
